## Q1
**Question:** Do you need to know coding to become a Solutions Architect at AWS?

**Answer:** No. As a Solutions Architect at AWS, we are not even allowed to code at customer's project. If the customer needs AWS folks to do hands-on keyboard coding, they need to pay money and the group that does it is Professional Services. When going for a job in AWS, the job description will clearly say if it is for Professional Services.

## Q2
**Question:** Will coding knowledge have any impact on the interview questions asked?

**Answer:** No. If you mention that you know coding or you came from a coding background, that does not impact the questions that the interviewer is going to ask. There is no coding challenge or data structure and algorithm round for AWS SA interviews.

## Q3
**Question:** Do you need a computer science degree to get into AWS as a Solutions Architect?

**Answer:** No. Not only you don't need a computer science degree, you don't even sometimes need an engineering degree. Experience in IT is what matters, not the degree. The interview outcome is determined solely by the interview answers you are giving.

## Q4
**Question:** Will the interviewer ask a fixed number of questions in an AWS SA interview?

**Answer:** No. Even though there is a foundational list of questions, most of the times interviewers will dive deep based on your answers and your resume. If you mention fancy projects, they will dive deep on those and expect explanations.

## Q5
**Question:** If you answer one or two questions wrong, will you be rejected?

**Answer:** No. AWS interviewers want to see how deep you can go. If you answer foundational questions correctly, even if you give wrong answers to a few questions, it is totally fine.

## Q6
**Question:** Do AWS interviewers start with complex corner case questions?

**Answer:** No. AWS interviewers believe in peeling the layer and understanding how much you know. Most candidates are not able to answer foundational scaling, compute, storage, security, and networking questions.

## Q7
**Question:** If you can answer some scaling questions correctly, what will the interviewer do next?

**Answer:** The interviewer will dive deeper to see how much you know about that topic.

## Q8
**Question:** What is a common mistake candidates make when answering Kubernetes scaling questions?

**Answer:** Candidates talk about regular EC2 scaling when asked how Kubernetes workloads scale, but in reality Kubernetes EC2s scale differently than vanilla EC2s.

## Q9
**Question:** How should you answer the "Tell me about yourself" question in an interview?

**Answer:** It should be a 2 to 3 minutes crisp answer highlighting top projects and referring to how it can help in the current role. Don't elaborate on all past work experiences. Treat it like an action-packed movie trailer, not the entire movie.

## Q10
**Question:** What should you do if your answer to "Tell me about yourself" is 5 minutes or longer?

**Answer:** Make it succinct and practice before going to the interview.

## Additional Study Notes

### Key Misconceptions Addressed:
1. **Coding Requirement Myth**: Solutions Architects at AWS don't code - that's handled by Professional Services
2. **CS Degree Requirement Myth**: Neither CS degree nor engineering degree is mandatory
3. **Fixed Questions Myth**: Interview structure adapts based on resume and answers
4. **One Wrong Answer Myth**: Foundational concepts matter more than perfection on every question
5. **Starting with Complex Questions Myth**: Interviewers start with foundational concepts

### Key Tips:
- Take ownership of what you say on your resume
- Lying about experience will lead to follow-up questions you cannot answer
- Most candidates fail at foundational concepts, not complex scenarios
- Verify the credibility of study materials before learning
- Practice the "Tell me about yourself" response to be 2-3 minutes maximum
